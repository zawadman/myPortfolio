About Me

Hi—I’m Zawad Ul Mutawassit, a Computer Science (B.Sc.) graduate (Visual Computing & Games) with a minor in Math from Memorial University of Newfoundland. I love building immersive interactive systems and high-performance software, from C++ ECS shooters to real-time 3D simulations and dynamic web apps. In my machine-learning projects I’ve used ResNet-50 for robust feature extraction and combined it with region-proposal techniques to isolate foreground objects, then performed object detection end-to-end. I’m equally at home crafting clean low-level game engines in Unreal 5 or building responsive UIs with React.js and Three.js. Most recently, I built a goal-based zombie shooter simulation in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprints to showcase AI behavior; you can view the demo video in the Projects section below. I recently built a goal-driven zombie shooter in UE5 to demonstrate AI simulation using Blueprints and dynamic interaction logic. I thrive on tackling complex problems with efficient, readable code—whether optimizing AI pipelines or mentoring peers—and I’m always excited to collaborate on innovations in graphics, vision, and intelligent systems.
